World Museum Collections

Discover Renaissance masterpieces in the world's greatest museums

The Renaissance legacy lives on in museums around the world, where masterpieces created centuries ago continue to inspire millions of visitors each year. These institutions preserve and celebrate the artistic achievements that defined an era and transformed Western culture forever.

The Metropolitan Museum of Art

New York City, United States

Founded in 1870, The Met is one of the world's largest and most comprehensive art museums. Its Renaissance collection spans European painting, sculpture, and decorative arts, showcasing the evolution of artistic techniques and humanist ideals across two centuries.

Paris, France

The world's most visited museum, the Louvre houses over 35,000 works of art including the most famous Renaissance painting ever created. Its Italian Renaissance collection is unparalleled, featuring works by Leonardo, Raphael, and the great Venetian masters.

The British Museum

London, United Kingdom

Established in 1753, the British Museum holds one of the world's finest collections of Renaissance prints and drawings. Its collection demonstrates the spread of Renaissance ideals across Europe, from Italian masters to Northern European innovators.
